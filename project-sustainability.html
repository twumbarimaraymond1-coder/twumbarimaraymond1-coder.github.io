Sustainable engineering coursework · Module 25WSC602

Life-cycle energy analysis of a consumer product

Type: Group project (disassembly + LCA) Tools: Ansys Granta EduPack Focus: embodied energy · eco-design
Overview. A four-person eco-design study of an HP OfficeJet Pro inkjet printer (Group E1), each member owning a chapter — disassembly, metallic recycling, non-metallic recycling and energy. My chapter was Energy Conservation: a whole-life energy assessment building a full material mass breakdown, calculating embodied and processing energy for every material in Ansys Granta EduPack, and identifying the most energy-intensive life-cycle phases to drive recommendations.

Method

1 · Material mass breakdown

Rather than estimating composition by hand during disassembly, I used HP's official Product Material Content document together with the product's 8.1 kg mass to compute the mass of each material accurately — from the dominant ABS housing down to trace copper and ferrite.

2 · Embodied & processing energy

For each material I drew embodied-energy and processing-energy figures from Ansys Granta EduPack and combined them with the calculated masses. For example, ABS carries an embodied energy of roughly 88.9–98.3 MJ/kg plus polymer-moulding processing of ~19.7–21.7 MJ/kg — and because ABS is also the largest mass fraction, it dominates the production-phase energy. The same method was applied across all materials and aggregated.

3 · Life-cycle phases & eco-design

I assessed each phase — raw materials and production, distribution, use, and end-of-life — to find where energy demand concentrates, then proposed targeted improvements. The work fed into a group disassembly study, eco-design considerations and a Pugh-matrix evaluation of design alternatives, set against circular-economy principles (remanufacturing, material recycling and energy recovery) covered in the module.

Skills demonstrated

  • Structured life-cycle assessment of a real product, end to end.
  • Quantitative materials and energy analysis using Ansys Granta EduPack.
  • Translating analysis into actionable eco-design and end-of-life recommendations.
  • Applying circular-economy thinking and structured decision tools (Pugh matrix) in a team.
