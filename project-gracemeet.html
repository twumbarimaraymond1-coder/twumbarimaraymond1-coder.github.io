GraceMeet — faith-centred matchmaking, powered by AI

Role: Founder & full-stack developer Stack: PWA · Supabase · Claude (Anthropic) Live: gracemeetapp.com
What it is. GraceMeet is an AI-powered Christian/Catholic matchmaking platform focused on lasting marriages rather than endless swiping. I founded it and built it end to end — product, full-stack engineering, AI integration, and the surrounding company and ethics work.
Visit gracemeetapp.com →

The idea

Most dating apps optimise for engagement, not outcomes. GraceMeet takes the opposite stance: a small number of deeply-considered, faith-aligned matches each week, with the explicit goal of helping people find a marriage. There is no swiping — users receive curated matches with a detailed compatibility breakdown and simply accept or pass.

What I built

  • A full progressive web app — onboarding, curated matching, accept/pass flow, real-time messaging, and installable/offline support via a service worker and web manifest.
  • An AI matching engine — compatibility analysis driven by Claude (Anthropic), called securely through a backend proxy so no keys ever touch the client.
  • A data model on Supabase — Postgres for profiles, faith and lifestyle details, matches, conversations and outcomes, with authentication and serverless edge functions.

The differentiator — a self-improving feedback loop

The part I'm most proud of is that GraceMeet is designed to get smarter over time. After a match, the app schedules automatic check-ins at milestones (two weeks, one month, three months and beyond) and captures structured ratings and free-text feedback on how the relationship is going. A weekly job then has the AI read across all of those outcomes, extract statistical patterns about what actually leads to healthy, lasting relationships, and feed those learnings back into future match generation.

In other words, every couple the system pairs makes the next round of matching a little better — a closed loop from outcome back to recommendation, which most static dating apps simply don't have.

Beyond the code

Building a product of this scope solo was only realistic because I lean on AI-assisted development — using LLMs to move fast across the full stack while keeping ownership of the architecture and decisions. As a solo founder I've also owned the things engineering courses don't teach: registering the company, writing the privacy policy, terms and community guidelines, thinking carefully about data security, and outreach to potential early communities. I care a lot about building this ethically — handling sensitive personal and faith data responsibly, and designing for genuine human dignity rather than addictive engagement.

What it demonstrates

  • End-to-end product ownership — from idea and data model to a deployed, installable application.
  • Full-stack web development and practical integration of large language models into a real product.
  • Backend and database design (Supabase / Postgres), authentication and serverless functions.
  • Product thinking, ethics and the commercial/operational side of getting something off the ground alone.
