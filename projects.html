Final-year individual project · 25WSC500

Predicting boiling heat flux with machine learning

Investigated whether data-driven methods could predict local boiling heat flux from high-speed infrared thermography. Processed a 17.4-million-sample dataset, applied Proper Orthogonal Decomposition and Dynamic Mode Decomposition, and trained three neural networks of increasing physical sophistication. The reduced-order modal model reached R² = 0.73 — a 69% gain in explained variance over the linear baseline.

Numerical heat transfer · 25WSC801

2-D steady conduction by finite differences

Derived energy-balance equations for interior, convective and cut-out boundary nodes, then implemented an iterative Jacobi solver for a generating bar section. Verified accuracy with a domain energy balance and a grid-refinement study, driving the imbalance from 0.87% down to −0.06%.

Finite element analysis · 25WSC106 · Group 21

Determining the safe working load of a wrench

A six-person study finding a spanner's safe working load by five independent methods — analytical, open-source Python, SimScale, ANSYS, Abaqus and physical testing. My contribution was the Python route: a 2D beam idealisation and a full 3D tetrahedral FEA (gmsh + scikit-fem), reaching 112.2 MPa peak stress at the jaw–handle transition.
