About me

Raymond Twum-Barima

Mechanical engineer in the making, with a habit of taking on more than the coursework asks for — in the lab and out of it.

I like problems that live between physics, simulation and data — and I like being able to explain why the answer is what it is.

The short version

I’m a final-year MEng Mechanical Engineering student at Loughborough University, currently finishing my degree after a year in industry at the UK Atomic Energy Authority. My interests sit firmly in thermofluids and computational engineering: finite element analysis, CFD, numerical methods, and increasingly the use of machine learning to make sense of experimental data.

At UKAEA I worked on structural and thermal FEA of cryogenic components for fusion research — modelling how parts behave at −196 °C and correlating simulations with physical tests. It taught me how real engineering happens: carefully, to documented standards, and always with validation against reality. That mindset runs through everything on my projects page.

What I care about in my work

  • Verification over assumption. A number isn’t a result until I’ve checked it — energy balances, convergence studies, comparison against experiment.
  • Understanding, not just output. I’d rather know which physical mechanism drives a result than have a black box that happens to be right.
  • Communicating clearly. A year of technical reporting and presenting to multidisciplinary teams taught me that an analysis only matters if other people can follow it.
  • Working with AI, openly. I use large-language-model tools as part of my everyday workflow — to write and debug code and speed up analysis — and I build AI-powered systems, from my machine-learning research to GraceMeet. I'm upfront about it, and I stay the engineer who makes the decisions and checks the work.

Beyond engineering

I’ve spent as much time leading and building communities as I have in the lab. I founded and chair my university’s Catholic Society, growing it from nothing — budgeting, events, committee, the lot. Before that I was Head Boy at school, which gave me an early taste of representing people and speaking in public.

Outside all of that, I’m a runner (I log most of it on Strava) — it keeps me consistent and gives me goals to chase. I sing in a choir, which is the other half of being comfortable in front of an audience. And I’m a bit of a history nerd: documentaries, and far too many hours in historical strategy games like EU4.

Where I’m headed

I’m looking for graduate and summer engineering opportunities — ideally in thermofluids, FEA or simulation, where I can keep working on hard physical problems with good people. If that sounds like your team, I’d love to hear from you.
